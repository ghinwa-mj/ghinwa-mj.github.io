Mother

Ghinwa Moujaes
Apr 10, 2020

On Heroes

As I was growing up
My favorite Super Hero was my Mother
Actually, it was Blossom from powerpuff girls
But it should have been my mother
Because
No one describes heroism better than
The person who pretends she doesn’t want that last piece of cake
Though she is starving
And that cake looks so damn delicious
And I know this feels like something you may have read off Facebook
Maybe because I read it off Facebook myself
Forgive me Mother but
It always baffles me how I can find the perfect poem
To talk about love
In all its different shapes and sizes
Except when it comes to you
It baffles me how I can find the perfect words
In every situation
In every occasion
But no words I find are enough
When it comes to
Describing how heroic you are, mother
Describing how beautiful your smile is, mother
Describing how you are the definition of a blessing, mother
You smile is contagious
It overflows
Every time I see you cry
Because the world is too messed up
Or because someone just died in the movie you were watching
Or because you were watching the news and Lebanon is breaking your heart
g to my room to talk to me and I just wasn’t in a good enough
Everytime I see you cry
I wish
More than anything
That I could be the hero that you are
And chase that sadness away from you
And ruin every little thing that makes you sad
Your resilience is contagious
It overflows
You grew up at a time where it took
Risking your life to go from one town to another
You grew up at a time where it took
Risking your life in order to get your college degree
Yet, you are not angry
Not bitter
You wake up everyday looking for the sunshine
In every ounce of darkness
You wake up everyday as a form of praise
Regarding a life filled with struggle
Yet you still eloquently win the battle
Against the darkness of the world
Your love is contagious
It overflows
Everytime I feel like my life is black and grey
You are red
You are pink and you are radiant
And every shiny color there is
Mother, I apologize for every single time
I was too blind to see
The cape you wear on your shoulder
Every morning after you wake up at 6am
And the halo you wear on your head
Every night after you spend the day
Fighting for goodness
Against evil
Mother, I am sorry that every time they used to ask me
Who my favorite super hero was
I was too blind to say it was my mother.
